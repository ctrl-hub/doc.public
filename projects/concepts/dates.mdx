---
title: 'Anticipated / Scheduled Dates'
---

Within projects, we have two types of dates: _anticipated_ and _scheduled_.

Schemes and Work Orders can have an "anticipated" start and end date. It is anticipated because it has not yet been _scheduled_.

Operations do not have anticipated dates, they have scheduled dates.

When an operation's scheduled date is changed, that change propagates to the work order and scheme - if the start date of the operation you are changing is before any of the other operations in the work order, the work order's start date will be updated.

Likewise, if the end date of the operation you are changing is after any of the other operations in the work order, the work order's end date will be updated.

This also propagates to the scheme, but the scheme considers the start and end date of all the work orders in it and their operations.

You can edit the anticipated dates of the work order and scheme, but you cannot edit the scheduled dates - to edit the scheduled dates, you must edit the operation's scheduled dates.
